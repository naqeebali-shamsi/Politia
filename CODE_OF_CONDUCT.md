# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Politia is a civic technology project. Its purpose is to make public data about elected representatives accessible and understandable. We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone who contributes, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are also committed to something additional that most open-source projects do not need to worry about: **political neutrality**. This project touches sensitive political data in a democracy with deeply held political opinions. The project's credibility depends on contributors keeping their political views out of the codebase, issues, pull requests, and community discussions.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, constructive, and professional in all interactions.
- Focus feedback on the work, not the person.
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully.
- Prioritize data accuracy and source integrity in all contributions.
- Use precise, neutral language when discussing politicians, parties, or political events.
- Assume good faith in other contributors' intentions.
- Credit others' work and ideas.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.
- Partisan political commentary in issues, PRs, code comments, or community channels. ("Party X is ruining the country" has no place here, even if you believe it strongly.)
- Deliberately introducing bias into data, scoring formulas, or UI language to favor or harm any politician or party.
- Using the project's data or platform to defame, target, or campaign against individuals.
- Publishing private information (doxxing) of any person, including politicians beyond what appears in official public records.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome advances.
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above.

### Political Neutrality in Practice

This deserves special emphasis. Contributors will inevitably have political opinions. That is normal and healthy. But this project is not the venue for expressing them.

- **In issues and PRs:** Discuss data accuracy, code quality, and user experience. Do not discuss whether a politician's actions are good or bad.
- **In code comments:** Describe what the code does, not what you think about the data it processes.
- **In commit messages:** Describe the change. "Fix entity resolution for Bihar MPs" is correct. "Fix the data that makes Party X look bad" is not.
- **In UI copy:** Present facts. "Declared 3 criminal cases in 2024 affidavit" is correct. Any phrasing that implies a moral judgment is not.

If a contributor repeatedly introduces partisan framing — even subtly — maintainers will address it. Persistent violations will result in removal from the project.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces: the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other communication channels associated with Politia. It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it by emailing the project maintainers. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

Reports should include:

- Your contact information
- Names (real or usernames) of those involved
- Description of the behavior and why you consider it unacceptable
- Links to relevant issues, PRs, or messages if applicable
- Any additional context

### Enforcement Actions

Maintainers will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

1. **Correction** — A private written warning with clarity on what was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
2. **Warning** — A formal warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the affected parties for a specified period.
3. **Temporary Ban** — Temporary removal from all project interaction and communication for a specified period.
4. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal from the project for severe or repeated violations.

### What Happens to Biased Code

If a contribution is found to introduce political bias — whether in data handling, scoring, UI language, or documentation — the contribution will be reverted regardless of its other merits. This is not a punishment; it is a quality control measure. The contributor is welcome to resubmit with the bias removed.
